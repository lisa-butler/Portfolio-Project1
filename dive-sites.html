Shore Dives in Donegal

St. Johns Point

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Steps
  • Depth: 27m+

  • How to dive

    A set of steps and slip provide easy entrance to the water. Once in the water swim out approximately 50m on the surface. At low tide the top of the wall breaks the surface, aim for that. Drop here and swim north easterly to dive the wall. Plenty of life here in summer. Some boulder fields at 15m if you venture away from the wall.

  • Specific Interest

    20m wall with plenty of life. Trigger fish in summer months

  • Best time to dive

    Mid tide, although can be dived at any tide. Night dive here in summer can be spectacular

Malin Beg

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Easy
  • Acessibility: Steps
  • Depth: 4-18m

  • How to dive

    The bay runs East-West. Swim to the stack on shore and drop here. A passage cave is present on the right side of the bay. This goes under the cliff and opens to a small bay in a collapsed cave, open to the sky and visible from near the parking location.

  • Specific Interest

    Sea stack, cave, octopus at night, thornback rays, swim throughs

  • Best time to dive

    Mid tide, although can be dived at any tide and is often quite sheltered. Night dive for octopus

  • Map location

    Link to Google Maps

Shore Dives in Sligo

Mullaghmore Thumb

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Rocks
  • Depth: 25m+

  • How to dive

    Thumb is located just out from a small pull in area. Swim approx 200m out and the top of thumb will be visible on low tides. Drop here and the wall should greet you. An out and back dive with some overhangs, a chimney swim through and lots of life.

  • Specific Interest

    Trigger fish is summer occassionally. Chimney from 18m to 6m. Overhangs and interesting rock formations

  • Best time to dive

    Low tide in calm weather as mullaghmore can be quite exposed. Also means there are less rocks to climb across

    Link to Google Maps

Mullaghmore Harbour

  • Dive Type: Harbour
  • Dive Skill: Easy
  • Acessibility: Steps
  • Depth: 1-5m

  • How to dive

    The dive is around the harbour wall. Enter via the last set of steps on the harbour and drop here. Swim out of the harbour mouth keeping to the wall to avoid boats. Follow the wall out as far as the pontoon.

  • Specific Interest

    Plumous anenomies, cuttlefish at night, eel

  • Best time to dive

    High tide, ideally on a spring tide. Can be dived in rougher weather depending on swell direction, however, due to the sandy bottom visability can deteriorate quickly

Shore Dives in Mayo

Killary Harbour

  • Dive Type: Harbour
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Slip
  • Depth: 4-25m+

  • How to dive

    Enter via the slip and swim straight out in line with the harbour wall into the fjord. There are two bouys approximately 300m into the fjord. Drop on the inner one and fin up the fjord away from the mouth. You should be in a channel approx 16m deep, follor this for 5 minutes and you will come upon the wreck of a wooden fishing boat.

  • Specific Interest

    Lots of life, scallops, wreck, fjord dive

  • Best time to dive

    Slack tide or high tide is ideal

Shore Dives in Clare

The Bridges of Ross

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Hard
  • Acessibility: Rocks
  • Depth: 30m+

  • How to dive

    Drop into the bay and fin out on the surface. Easy wall dive with beautiful life to the left of the bay. Alternatively, swim out to the mouth of the bay on the right and drop here between the rocks. You will find two arches immediately at 10m, follow the rock wall out with it on your right and you will encounter a boulder field with many swim throughs.

  • Specific Interest

    Arches, caves, boulder fields, avenues

  • Best time to dive

    Any tide. However a higher tide reduces time walking across the rocks

Miltown Malbay

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Rocks
  • Depth: 6-17m+

  • How to dive

    There is a dive to the left or the right of the bay. The right side features a 15m wall with lots of dog fish and small rock swim throughs. Find this by following a gully that runs straight out of the bay and dropping by the rock that sticks above the surface even at high tide. To the left you will also fund a gully to follow out. This will lead you eventually to two sea arches at 15m and some rock avenues.

  • Specific Interest

    Arches, dolphin, swim throughs

  • Best time to dive

    Any tide and can still be dived in rough conditions often

  • Map location

    Link to Google Maps
