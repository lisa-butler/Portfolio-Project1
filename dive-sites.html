Shore Dives in Donegal

St. Johns Point

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Steps and field
  • Depth: 27m+

  • How to dive

    From the carpark a short walk across a field (often complete with cow poop) and down a set of stone steps brings you to a slip and easy entrance to the water. Once in the water swim out approximately 50m on the surface. At low tide the top of the wall breaks the surface, aim for that. Drop here and swim north easterly to dive the wall. Plenty of life here in summer. Some boulder fields at 15m if you venture away from the wall.

  • Specific Interest

    20m wall with plenty of life. Trigger fish in summer months.

  • Best time to dive

    Mid tide, although can be dived at any tide. Night dive here in summer can be spectacular.

Malin Beg

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Easy
  • Acessibility: Steps
  • Depth: 4-18m

  • How to dive

    The bay runs East-West. Swim to the stack on shore and drop here. Plenty to see on the stack including kelp swim throughs and lots of life. A passage cave is present on the right side of the bay, slightly further than the stack on the way out. This goes under the cliff and opens to a small bay in a collapsed cave, open to the sky and visible from near the parking location.

  • Specific Interest

    Sea stack, cave, octopus at night, thornback rays, swim throughs.

  • Best time to dive

    Mid tide, although can be dived at any tide and is often quite sheltered. Night dive for octopus.

Shore Dives in Sligo

Mullaghmore Thumb

  • Dive Type: Shore
  • Dive Skill: Medium
  • Acessibility: Rough rocks
  • Depth: 25m+

  • How to dive

    Park in the pull in area just through mullaghmore village on the way around the scenic drive. Thumb is located just out from here. Climb across the rocks and swim approx 200m out to a position where the pier head hotel lines up with the end of benbulben and the wooden telegraph pole is in line with the skylight on the stone house. The top of thumb is visible on low tides. Drop here and the wall should greet you. An out and back dive with some overhangs, a chimney swim through and lots of life.

  • Specific Interest

    Trigger fish is summer occassionally. Chimney from 18m to 6m. Overhangs and interesting rock formations.

  • Best time to dive

    Mid tide in calm weather as mullaghmore can be quite exposed. Also means there are less rocks to climb across.
